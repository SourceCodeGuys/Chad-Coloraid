What is Colorblindness?

Colorblindness, or color vision deficiency, is the inability to perceive colors in a way that is typical of most people. There are several types, each affecting the perception of different colors. Common types of color vision deficiencies include Protanopia 'PROTAN', Deuteranopia 'DEUTAN', and Tritanopia 'TRITAN'.

Our Story

ColorAid began as an undergraduate project aimed at addressing one of the most common yet overlooked challenges: color perception deficiency (CPD). Observing the difficulties faced by those with CPD, our team decided to leverage existing technologies and create a robust, adaptive tool that enhances color discernment. By combining the power of the Farnsworth-Munsell Hue Test with an innovative ensemble of LMS, CBFS, and LAB algorithms, our web application adapts real-time to the user's specific needs, offering a unique, customized visual experience.

Mission

At ColorAid, our mission is to enhance the visual experience of individuals with color perception deficiencies through innovative technological solutions. By integrating advanced algorithms into a user-friendly web application, we are dedicated to improving accessibility and enabling our users to perceive the world in vibrant color. Our goal is to empower color-deficient individuals to overcome visual challenges, enhancing both their professional capabilities and quality of life.
